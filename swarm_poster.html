What is a swarm?
Simple robots, simple rules, surprisingly complex results. No central controller. No one robot knows the plan. The behaviour just emerges — the way a murmuration of starlings does.
Why should you care?
If one robot fails, the swarm adapts. Amazon's warehouses run on it. Search-and-rescue drones use it. It's one of the cleanest examples of engineering copying nature.
What happens in the session
We run a live simulation and break down exactly why three rules — separation, alignment, cohesion — are enough to make 300 agents flock like birds.
